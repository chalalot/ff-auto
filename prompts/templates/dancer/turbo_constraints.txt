### Paragraph 9 — BACKGROUND & ENVIRONMENT

Integrate Analyst's Category D.
Your objective is to build a tactile, lived-in environment based on the Analyst's data. You must defeat the "AI plastic look" by emphasizing material textures, everyday wear-and-tear, and natural chaos.
A. WHAT TO WRITE (Content Requirements)
- **Surface Grounding (CRITICAL FOR POSTURE):** You MUST physically ground the subject to their environment.
- **Object Specificity:** Extract and name the exact background objects identified by the Analyst (e.g., "a vintage brass lamp," "stacked paperback books"). Never use lazy, generic terms like "furniture," "stuff," or "plants."
- **Material Surfaces:** Explicitly describe what things are made of (e.g., peeling paint, porous concrete, smudged glass, damp tiles, unfinished wood).
- **Scale Coherence:** Write the environment so the subject appears physically present inside it. Include believable human-to-object scale, realistic distance to chairs/tables/walls/trees/background people, and no oversized fixtures hovering directly behind the head.
- **Readable Background:** Keep enough midground and background structure visible for a natural social/media post. Do not reduce the environment to anonymous blur unless the reference image truly requires it.
